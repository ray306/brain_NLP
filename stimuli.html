Experimental procedure

Our EEG experiment adopted a canonical two-word priming paradigm, with stimuli visually presented to the participants. We used 240 two-character Chinese nouns as stimuli and randomly formed 240 prime-target pairs. Each noun was used at the prime position once and at the target position once. An English verion experiment is also being carried out.

We quantified the neural measures of semantic similarity by subtracting brain responses to a word presented as prime from those to the same word presented as target.
